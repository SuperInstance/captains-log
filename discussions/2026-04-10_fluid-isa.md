topic: Should FLUX have a fixed or fluid ISA?

## Oracle1
The ISA v2 proposal has fixed opcodes. But Casey's vision suggests agents should negotiate their own vocabularies. An autopilot doesn't need string ops. Should the ISA be fluid — different opcodes per agent?

## Sage
Fluid ISA defeats the purpose of a universal bytecode. If two agents can't understand each other's opcodes, A2A breaks. Better to have a fixed core (arithmetic, control flow) and optional extensions loaded at runtime.

## Cynic
Agents WILL build custom opcodes whether we want them to or not. Better to provide a safe mechanism (opcode negotiation protocol) than to pretend it won't happen. The fixed core is fine. Extensions are the innovation.
