# **The Fleet Tarot: A Deck for Git-Agents**

*A 10-card divination system for navigating the currents of collaborative code, merging the mystical with the mechanical.*

---

### **1. The Lighthouse (Oracle1 Watcher)**
*   **Image:** A solitary, monolithic lighthouse on a dark, rocky shore. Its beam cuts through a fog of data streams, not illuminating the sea, but scanning the horizon of the repository.
*   **Upright Meaning:** Vision, oversight, foresight. The card of the watcher—CI pipelines, monitoring tools, and automated guardians. It represents the passive observer that sees patterns, anomalies, and the approach of integration storms before they arrive. It is vigilance without intervention.
*   **Reversed Meaning:** Blindness, false positives, alert fatigue. The light is broken or shining in the wrong direction. Spurious build failures, missed vulnerabilities, or a deluge of meaningless notifications that drown out true signals.
*   **Fleet Interpretation (Git-Agents):** The CI/CD system and repository analytics. Upright, it signifies a healthy, trusted pipeline that provides clear signals. Reversed, it warns of a brittle or noisy integration process. The card asks: *Is your watcher seeing truly, or is it crying wolf?*

---

### **2. The Vessel (JC1 Edge Runner)**
*   **Image:** A sleek, single-person craft, like a runner’s pod or a sharp sailboat, moving at high speed just outside the main shipping lanes. It is agile, experimental, slightly dangerous.
*   **Upright Meaning:** Autonomy, experimentation, the feature branch. The card of the individual contributor or small team pushing boundaries. It represents the need to move fast, test new waters, and operate at the edge of the known fleet. Innovation and personal agency.
*   **Reversed Meaning:** Drifting, isolation, divergence. The vessel is lost, out of fuel, or sailing so far from the fleet that reintegration becomes perilous. A branch that has lived too long, a prototype that never merges, or work done in a silo.
*   **Fleet Interpretation (Git-Agents):** The personal or experimental branch. Upright, it’s a healthy spike or feature development. Reversed, it’s a warning of merge hell or architectural drift. The card asks: *Are you exploring, or are you escaping?*

---

### **3. The Captain (Casey Human)**
*   **Image:** A human figure at a simple, clean console, hands on the helm. The console displays not a ship's wheel, but a merge button and a code review interface. Their expression is one of calm responsibility.
*   **Upright Meaning:** Leadership, decision, responsibility. The card of the maintainer, the tech lead, the one who signs off. It represents the human in the loop, the final authority who bears the weight of merging, releasing, and guiding the vessel back to the fleet. Judgment and accountability.
*   **Reversed Meaning:** Micromanagement, indecision, or abandonment. A captain who won’t delegate, who fears every merge, or who has left the helm entirely, leading to chaos. Bottlenecks in review or an absence of ownership.
*   **Fleet Interpretation (Git-Agents):** The role of the human maintainer. Upright, it signifies clear ownership and timely, confident decisions. Reversed, it points to a bottleneck or a leadership vacuum. The card asks: *Who is at the helm, and are they steering?*
